Cyclone Preparedness

Tropical storms and hurricane-force winds

High Risk
Warning Signs
  • Sudden drop in air pressure
  • Strong winds and dark clouds
  • Weather alerts from authorities
Immediate Actions
  • Stay indoors and secure windows
  • Keep emergency kit ready
  • Avoid coastal areas
Community Safety Tips
  • Help neighbors prepare
  • Share verified alerts only
  • Follow established evacuation routes
Emergency Contacts
  • 1078 - National Disaster Helpline
  • 112 - Emergency Services

Flood Safety

Coastal and urban flash-flood preparedness

High Risk
Warning Signs
  • Unusually heavy sustained rainfall
  • Rapidly rising water levels in drains and rivers
  • Flash flood warnings issued by meteorologists
Immediate Actions
  • Never walk or drive through flood waters
  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Turn off utilities at main switches if instructed
Community Safety Tips
  • Identify community high-ground routes
  • Assist elderly or disabled neighbors
  • Do not consume unboiled tap water
Emergency Contacts
  • 1070 - Regional Relief Helpline
  • 112 - Police and Fire Department

Landslide Risk Prevention

Debris flows, mudslides, and earth movement

Med Risk
Warning Signs
  • Leaning trees, new cracks, or shifting ground
  • A rumbling sound that increases in volume
  • Sudden surges or drops in stream water levels
Immediate Actions
  • Move away from the slide path if possible
  • If escape is not possible, curl into a tight ball
  • Stay alert during severe rainstorms
Community Safety Tips
  • Plant ground cover on slopes to strengthen terrain
  • Familiarize yourself with local landslide paths
  • Share structural damage reports with authorities
Emergency Contacts
  • 112 - Emergency Rescue
  • 101 - Local Fire Department

Marine Pollution Response

Chemical spills, oil leaks, and coastal hazards

Low Risk
Warning Signs
  • Strong chemical odors or visible slicks on water
  • Sudden illness among wildlife and fish
  • Unusual coloring along the coastline
Immediate Actions
  • Stay upwind of the affected area to avoid fumes
  • Do not touch or clean up unknown chemicals
  • Report the incident to environmental authorities
Community Safety Tips
  • Follow local water usage restrictions
  • Avoid consuming local seafood until declared safe
  • Never burn hazardous marine debris
Emergency Contacts
  • 1554 - Coast Guard Helpline
  • 112 - Hazmat Emergency Response
